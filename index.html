Sound familiar?

The tools that got you this far are now the thing slowing you down.

  • Orders and requests arrive by text, email, and phone, with no single place to track them.
  • The same information gets typed into three different systems by hand.
  • Two tools that should talk to each other don't, so someone bridges the gap manually.
  • A critical spreadsheet that only one person really understands.
  • A job your team repeats every week that a computer could do in seconds.
  • You know exactly how the business runs, but if you disappeared for a week, would anyone else?

Left alone, this doesn't settle down. It builds. The hours lost to copying data add up. Small mistakes surface at the worst possible moment. And the whole operation stays wired to one person's memory, which you feel every time you try to take a day off.

Here's the other side of it:

  • Every order and request lands in one place your whole team can see.
  • Information gets entered once and shows up everywhere it needs to.
  • Your tools talk to each other, so nobody has to play middleman.
  • What one person knows lives in the system, where the whole team can reach it.
  • The weekly busywork just happens, without anyone touching it.
  • You can step away for a week and the business keeps running.

If any of that feels less like an inconvenience and more like something you're bracing for, it's exactly the kind of thing I fix.
